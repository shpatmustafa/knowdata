Global Workforce
Integration.

Unifying operations for a global infrastructure and renewable energy leader to synchronise project-based labour costing across dispersed wind farms and solar arrays.

01 Strategic Imperative

The renewable energy and infrastructure sector operates on a project-based model that demands extreme agility. For this Global Renewable Energy Client, managing a workforce dispersed across remote wind farms and construction sites worldwide presented a critical data challenge.

The Challenge

Rapid expansion through acquisitions created a fragmented landscape of legacy systems. Labour costs and safety certifications were siloed in regional databases, hindering accurate bidding and exposing the organisation to safety compliance risks.

The Objective

Deploy a unified Workday environment serving as the single source of truth, connecting HR operations with critical downstream systems to enhance transparency and operational efficiency globally.

02 Solution Architecture

The scope of this engagement was massive, requiring the orchestration of over **70 distinct integrations** to connect Workday with a diverse ecosystem of third-party applications.

Studio & Custom Connectors

Utilised Workday Studio to build bi-directional integrations with local payroll vendors. This ensured site allowances and hazard pay for field technicians were calculated accurately across multiple jurisdictions.

Project & Labour Costing

Synced time-tracking data directly into ERP systems (e.g., Oracle Primavera). This enabled real-time "actual vs. budget" spend tracking for every individual wind farm installation.

Safety & Compliance (EHS)

Built automated connectors to synchronise worker profiles with EHS systems. Site access is now automatically restricted if critical certifications (like GWO Working at Heights) have expired.

Agile Delivery Methodology

We adopted a distributed Agile delivery model to manage multi-stream parallel workstreams across time zones. Extensive "Parallel Run" testing ensured zero disruption to employee pay during the global cutover.
