Driving sales growth for Microsoft Ireland through a data-driven digital transformation that translated global product vision into local commercial success.

01 Strategic Imperative

For global technology giants, the challenge often lies in translating global product launches into locally relevant market interventions. While broad campaigns drive awareness, conversion requires a tailored approach that resonates with specific local buying behaviours.

The Challenge

Generic global assets were not optimised for the Irish digital ecosystem, leading to suboptimal conversion rates and disjointed customer journeys across critical partner channels like Dixons and Harvey Norman.

The Objective

Align digital touchpoints with commercial objectives, ensuring that the "digital shelf" in Ireland was as robust and compelling as the physical shelf to drive sales growth for Surface and M365.

02 Execution Framework

The engagement was structured around a rigorous **"Plan-Build-Execute"** framework, moving beyond vanity metrics to focus on hard commercial drivers.

Strategic Alignment

Facilitated intensive workshops with Sales, Marketing, and Product teams to create a unified **"Success Scorecard"** focused on Conversion Rate (CR) and Average Order Value (AOV).

Digital Ecosystem Overhaul

Localised the web presence by integrating Irish search trends and tailoring messaging to highlight local financing offers. We reduced friction in the "Learn to Buy" path through deep UX audits.

Omnichannel Partner Strategy

Established data sharing protocols with **Dixons (Currys)** and **Harvey Norman** to ensure digital shelf consistency. This ensured a high-quality, synchronised brand experience across all partner channels.
